Hostinger vs InterServer: Which Hosting Deal Is Actually Cheaper Over 3 Years?

Hostinger and InterServer can look different at checkout because hosting brands use different mixes of intro discounts, renewal rules, included features, and plan limits. Instead of guessing current prices, this guide gives you a three-year cost worksheet you can fill in with live prices before buying.

Summary
  • Compare total cost over 36 months, not only month-one pricing.
  • Hostinger-style intro discounts can be attractive when the first term is long and the plan fits.
  • InterServer-style comparisons should include renewal policy, included features, and support needs.
  • The cheaper choice depends on current checkout prices, renewal terms, and the add-ons you actually need.

Risk and hidden-cost comparison

  • Promotional anchoring: a large discount can hide a higher renewal rate. Confirm the renewal price before paying.
  • Resource limits: storage, CPU, inode, database, and site-count limits matter more than the headline plan name.
  • Support expectations: a small business site may value responsive support more than a few dollars of savings.
  • Upgrade path: if you may need VPS or managed WordPress later, compare upgrade pricing now.

Buyer checklist: choose the cheaper 3-year deal

  1. Calculate a 36-month total using live checkout and renewal prices.
  2. Circle add-ons you cannot skip: domain, email, backups, SSL features, malware cleanup, and staging.
  3. Check refund windows and cancellation rules for both providers.
  4. Compare performance needs: traffic, storage, database count, and number of websites.
  5. Pick the provider with the lowest realistic total cost for your actual site, not the best advertised discount.
