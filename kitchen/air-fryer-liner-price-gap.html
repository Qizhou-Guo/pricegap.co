What Is a Silicone Air Fryer Liner, and Why Does It Need Certification?

A silicone air fryer liner is a reusable, heat-resistant mat placed inside the air fryer basket. It prevents food from sticking and makes cleanup significantly easier. No coating to peel, no parchment paper to buy repeatedly.

The product is simple. But what it's made of — and whether it can safely contact food at high temperatures — is where regulation kicks in.

The Regulatory Standards That Actually Matter

Three food-contact material standards define what a safe silicone liner must meet. Each one has a real cost attached to compliance.

LFGB (Germany / EU — the strictest)

The Lebensmittel- und Futtermittelgesetzbuch is the EU gold standard for food contact materials. A liner with LFGB certification has been tested for organoleptic properties (no taste or odor transfer) and chemical migration at typical use temperatures. Most China factories that export to Europe pursue this.

Certification cost to factory: ¥8,000–¥25,000 per product, per year. This cost is amortized across units — but it still adds $0.50–$2.00 to the per-unit cost at volume.

FDA Food Contact Substances (US)

The US FDA 21 CFR Part 177 governs silicone rubber articles intended for food contact. A compliant silicone liner has been reviewed for safe use up to its rated temperature. Products sold on Amazon US are supposed to meet this — enforcement varies.

Certification cost to factory: ¥5,000–¥18,000 per product. Some AliExpress listings claim FDA compliance; not all have documentation.

China GB 4806 (Domestic Standard)

China's GB 4806.11-2016 sets the national standard for food-contact silicone rubber. Factories producing for domestic Chinese retail must meet this. The standard covers permissible ingredients, migration limits, and sensory requirements.

The practical implication: a China factory that already holds GB 4806 compliance can pursue LFGB or FDA certification at lower incremental cost than a non-compliant factory. This is why the certification gap between a ¥15 unbranded mat and a ¥30–¥45 LFGB mat isn't just about the certification fee — it's about the factory's overall quality system.

Certification caveat: Self-reported certification claims on AliExpress listings are not always verified. If compliance matters to you (it should for food contact products), filter by sellers with third-party test report uploads, or ask for the specific test certificate number before purchasing.
